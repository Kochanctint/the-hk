Health Check

Frequency of health checks depends on individual’s physical condition, as some may require one checkup each year, while some may require every 3-4 months. The following are common check up items.

Physical Examination

Examination of Heart, Lungs and Abdomen/Neurological and Musculoskeletal Examination/Height and Weight Measurements/Body Mass Index (BMI)/Visual Acuity and Colour Blindness Tests/Blood Pressure Reading

X- Ray, Ultrasound, EC Examinations

  • Chest X-ray
  • Spirometry
  • Tonometry
  • Resting Electrocardiogram (ECG)
  • Stress ECG (Treadmill)
  • Ultrasound Liver
  • Ultrasound Breast
  • Ultrasound Pelvis
  • Mammogram

Blood and Urine Examinations

  • - Total Cholesterol, HDL-Cholesterol, Total/HDL Cholesterol Ratio, LDL-Cholesterol, Triglycerides
  • -Bilirubin (Total), Alkaline Phosphatase, SGGT, SGPT (ALT), SGOT (AST), Total Protein, Albumin, Globulin, Albumin/Globulin Ratio, Hepatitis Bs Antigen, Hepatitis Bs Antibody, Hepatitis A lgG Antibody, Amylase
  • - Urea, Creatinine, Bicarbonate, Potassium, Sodium, Chloride
  • - Fasting Blood Sugar
  • - Calcium, Phosphates, Uric Acid, RA Factor
  • - VDRL
  • - PSA (Prostate, Men), CA 125 (Ovarian, Ladies)
  • - Free T4
  • - White Blood Count, Red Blood Count, Haemoglobin, PCV (HCT), Platelet, Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate, MCV, MCH, MCHC, Differential Count, Peripheral Blood Film, Blood Grouping (ABO & Rhesus)
  • - Urine Routine
  • - Stool Occult Blood
  • - Cervical Smear (For ladies)
